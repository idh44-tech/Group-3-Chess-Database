The London:

A very simple yet effective opening of moving the d2 pawn to d4 and then moving the c1 bishop to f4, it gives white a wide array of options of how to respond next to black.

Queens Gambit:

This opening positions two pawns in front of the queen one of which is to be sacrificed. The purpose of this is to get the queen into a good position.

Ruy Lopez:

The purpose of this opening is for white to develop control of the center and to build into a future pawn push.

This defense is used in response to the e4 pawn being moved by white and gives black opportunities later to advance the queen to be able to effectively defend many different strategies.

Kings Indian Defense:

This defense is useful in response to the d4 pawn for black to set up an agressive counter attack to white setting up in the center with a strong pawn formation.

French Defense:

This defense is another response to teh e4 pawn and gives many options on what to advance being the bishop and queen being very easy to advance from this position and leads to more symetry as both players have access to the same pieces as choices to advance.
